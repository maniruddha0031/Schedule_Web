KPI Definitions Handbook
All metrics and performance indicators used in this dashboard

Primary KPI Tiles

— Top row of the dashboard
Forecasted Sales

The projected total revenue for the current week, built from historical trends, day-of-week averages, and demand signals. It is the revenue target the entire schedule is built around.

Scheduled Labor Cost %

Total cost of all scheduled shifts expressed as a % of Forecasted Sales. Formula: (Scheduled Labor $) ÷ (Forecasted Sales) × 100. Target is typically 18–19% in QSR.

Scheduled Hours

Total employee-hours scheduled for the current week across all shifts and departments. Helps managers see total labor volume at a glance.

Scheduled SPLH

Sales Per Labor Hour — forecasted revenue generated for each scheduled labor hour. Formula: Forecasted Sales ÷ Scheduled Hours. Higher = more revenue per labor hour (greater efficiency).

Compliance

% of scheduled shifts fully compliant with labor laws and company policies (meal breaks, rest periods, minor-hour limits, OT thresholds). Formula: (Compliant Shifts ÷ Total Shifts) × 100.

AI Schedule Summary

A composite optimization score (0–100 / letter grade) from the AI engine. Evaluates the schedule against forecast demand, labor targets, skill balance, compliance, and coverage gaps — surfacing actionable alerts.

Secondary KPI Blocks

— Second row of the dashboard
Upcoming Shifts

Count of shifts scheduled to start within the next 2–4 hours for the current day. Useful for monitoring who is about to clock in.

Currently In

Number of employees actively clocked in on a shift right now. Gives an instant headcount of live floor coverage.

Today's Sch. Hrs.

Total scheduled labor hours for the current calendar day across all employees and all departments.

Weekly Sch. Hrs.

Total scheduled labor hours across the entire current workweek (Mon–Sun). Compared against the labor budget to spot over/under-scheduling.

Today OT

Overtime hours accumulated today — hours beyond the daily OT threshold (typically 8 hrs/day). Triggers premium pay at 1.5× rate.

Weekly OT

Total overtime hours accumulated this week — hours beyond the weekly OT threshold (typically 40 hrs/week). Key driver of unplanned labor cost.

Today $

Estimated labor dollar cost for all shifts scheduled today, calculated from scheduled hours × each employee's pay rate.

Weekly $

Estimated total labor dollar cost for the entire current workweek. Compared against the weekly labor budget to assess spend position.

Trend Graph Sub-Metrics

— Shown in KPI graph modals
Sales WTD

Actual sales revenue accumulated from Monday through today (Week-to-Date).

WTD Variance

Dollar difference between Actual Sales WTD and Forecasted Sales WTD. Negative = under-performing.

Actual Labor % WTD

Real labor cost % from actual clock-in/out data this week, vs. the scheduled labor % target.

Labor Variance

Difference between Scheduled Labor % and Actual Labor %. Positive = ran over scheduled target.

On-Time % WTD

% of shifts where the employee clocked in within the grace window (±5 min) week-to-date.

Missed Hours WTD

Total scheduled hours that went unfilled due to no-shows, early call-outs, or unassigned shifts week-to-date.

Actual SPLH WTD

Real SPLH from actual sales ÷ actual labor hours recorded so far this week. Compared to Scheduled SPLH target.

SPLH Variance

Dollar difference between Scheduled SPLH and Actual SPLH. Negative = each labor hour is generating less revenue than planned.

Compliance Violation Categories

— Compliance KPI breakdown
Meal Break — Employee worked beyond the legal/policy threshold (e.g., 5 hours) without a recorded meal break.
Minor Hours — An under-age employee scheduled beyond maximum permitted daily/weekly hours or during legally restricted hours (e.g., school nights).
OT Threshold — Employee is at or approaching the daily (8h) or weekly (40h) overtime threshold; next scheduled shift will likely incur premium pay.
Rest Period — Insufficient time between the end of one shift and the start of the next (e.g., less than 8 hours between clock-out and next clock-in).
No-Show — A scheduled shift where the employee did not clock in and no approved absence was recorded, resulting in an unfilled shift.

Weekly Operations Table Columns

— Bottom labor history table
Column Definition
DateThe calendar date of the operating day.
Labor %Actual labor cost as a % of actual sales for that day. The primary efficiency target for managers.
Forecasted SalesThe pre-set revenue target for that specific day based on historical and seasonal projections.
Actual SalesReported revenue recorded for that day, shown with ▲▼ % variance vs. the forecast target.
Sched. HoursTotal labor hours that were originally scheduled for that day before any changes.
Actual HoursTotal labor hours actually worked (from clock-in/out data) for that day.
Labor $Total dollar cost of labor paid for that day (regular + overtime pay combined).
Open ShiftsNumber of scheduled shifts that remained unassigned or unfilled for that day.

Note on SPLH: In QSR/fast-casual, SPLH is one of the most critical efficiency metrics. A store forecasting $12,000/day with 200 scheduled hours targets $60 SPLH. Below target = overstaffed relative to revenue; above target = risk of understaffing.
